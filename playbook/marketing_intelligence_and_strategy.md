# Playbook: Full-Stack AI Brand Content Strategy & Execution SOP

This playbook defines your content production schedule, platform routing channels, and templates for writing under the Jezreal Momoh brand.

---

## 1. Focused Distribution Model
To prevent surface-level presence, you are focusing solely on four active channels, routing all social media traffic to your owned newsletter audience:

```
LinkedIn (Daily)  <===>  X / Twitter (3-4x/week)
       \                  /
        \                /
        ▼                ▼
     Newsletter (Weekly Sunday recap)
         ▲
         │
     GitHub (Continuous Commits & Full-Stack Projects)
```

### Inactive Channels (Ignored to maintain focus):
*   **Instagram, TikTok, Threads, Facebook, Discord, Telegram**: Disabled. Do not spend operational energy creating, formatting, or posting content to these channels.

---

## 2. Platforms & Posting Roles

| Platform | Role | Frequency | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **LinkedIn** | Primary professional channel | Daily (8:00 AM WAT) | High-value, story-first posts to capture clients and founders. |
| **X (Twitter)** | Developer community chat | 3–4x per week | Blunt, opinionated, casual tech commentary; shorter versions of LinkedIn posts. |
| **Newsletter** | Owned audience hub (Substack/Beehiiv) | Weekly (Sunday evening) | A 10-minute recap: 1 thing built, 1 concept learned, 1 link shared, 1 reflection. |
| **GitHub** | Technical portfolio | Daily Commits | The anchor of your engineering credibility. All coding stories must link back here. |

---

## 3. Brand Tone & Formatting Checklists
Every piece of text must pass these rules before publishing:

*   **Proof of Work**: Every LinkedIn story post must link back to a specific GitHub commit, code repository, or technical architectural diagram to prevent the "AI Hype Man" perception.
*   **Newsletter Funnel**: Every third LinkedIn/X post must end with a Call to Action (CTA) inviting readers to subscribe to your weekly Substack/Beehiiv newsletter.
*   **Grammar**: No em dashes (—) under any circumstances. Use colons, commas, or parentheses instead.
*   **Tone**: Must sound like a real person talking. Avoid manufactured emotional buildup (e.g., *"I was crying in my car..."*).
*   **Slop Filter**: Delete corporate filler and AI-generated transitional phrases:
    *   *Deny list*: "genuinely", "honestly", "straightforward", "delve deeper", "crucial", "testament", "navigating".
*   **LinkedIn Format**: Short, punchy paragraphs, first-person singular, line breaks. Avoid bullet lists unless representing structured code concepts.
